## Primary Route: Trans-Dolomiti

**bikepacking.com (Tales on Tyres)** — 262 km loop, 9,529 m climbing. Ride clockwise from Brixen/Bressanone.

- Old WWI military roads (Strade Militari) over high passes
- Rocky dirt tracks, singletrack sections, alpine meadows between colossal limestone towers
- Hut-to-hut infrastructure available but wild camping is more flexible in Italy
- The Dolomite geology is extraordinary: Triassic carbonate reef platforms, now standing as sheer towers. **Enrosadira effect** — the rock turns pink/orange at dawn and dusk.

## Add-On: Zillertal Trail

**bikepacking.com** — 177 km loop, three passes connecting Italian and Austrian Zillertal Alps. Start from Brunico (on the Trans-Dolomiti route). Adds ~5 days if conditions allow.

## Key Logistics

- Mid-September timing is ideal: fewer hikers, passes still open, autumn colours starting
- Espresso and pasta at every resupply town
- Water from mountain streams and village fountains
- Bike shops in Brixen, Bolzano, Brunico — **get a full drivetrain service here** (~4,000 km on the drive train by this point)
- Book fork damper service in Bolzano/Brixen in advance — September is busy season

## Nature & Drawing Highlights

The Dolomites are among the most visually dramatic mountains on Earth. The rock formations change colour through the day — from grey-white at noon to deep amber/red at sunset (enrosadira). WWI remains throughout — trenches, cableways, ammunition stores built into the cliff faces. Extraordinary subject matter.
