**Fly:** London → Tbilisi (Wizz Air, typically £50–100)
~500–600 km, ~15,000 m climbing

## Route: Eastern Caucasus Highlight Loop

**Day-by-day framework:**

1. **Tbilisi → Stepantsminda** (1–2 days)
   - Georgian Military Highway, 155 km, paved. Heavy truck traffic in lower sections.
   - Consider marshrutka to Gudauri (~120 km) and riding the scenic upper section only — Jvari Pass (2,379 m) then descent to Stepantsminda.
   - Stops: Ananuri Fortress (65 km), Zhinvali Reservoir, Gudauri, Russia-Georgia Friendship Monument.

2. **Stepantsminda base** (1 day)
   - Ride up to Gergeti Trinity Church — 6 km, 430 m gain on rough 4×4 track. Good shakedown ride.
   - Sort logistics: check Abano Pass status, get trail info from Mountain Freaks office.

3. **Truso Valley out-and-back** (1–2 days)
   - Dirt road following the Terek gorge upstream. ~50 km round trip.
   - Highlight: travertine terraces with mineral springs — turquoise/orange calcium carbonate deposits, sulphur springs, abandoned village of Ketrisi.
   - Excellent for photography and drawing. The geology here is unique in the Caucasus.
   - Camp at the terraces or return to Stepantsminda.

4. **Juta Valley exploration** (1–2 days)
   - 15 km unpaved road from Sno Valley turnoff to Juta village (2,200 m).
   - Juta sits at the foot of the Chaukhi Massif — massive limestone towers sometimes called the "Dolomites of Georgia."
   - Ride/hike up the valley toward Chaukhi. Even if the pass is closed (it will be in late May), the approach is spectacular.
   - ⚠️ **Chaukhi Pass (3,340 m) does not open until mid-June.** Do not attempt without local confirmation from Mountain Freaks in Stepantsminda.

5. **Stepantsminda → Barisakho** (1 day)
   - Ride south over Jvari Pass, then east. ~90 km on paved roads.
   - Stock up in Barisakho: no shops for 70 km on the road to Shatili.

6. **Barisakho → Shatili** (1–2 days)
   - Rough 4×4 dirt road up the Argun gorge. ~70 km. Spectacular gorge riding.
   - Camp in the gorge or push through to Shatili.

7. **Shatili** (1 day)
   - Medieval fortified village — stacked stone towers forming a defensive complex.
   - Visit the Crypts of Anatori (short ride) — medieval communal tomb with skulls and bones.
   - Register at army checkpoint if required for the border area.

8. **Shatili → Alvani** (1 day, transfer)
   - Backtrack to Barisakho or arrange a transfer to the Alazani Valley.
   - Check in Alvani whether Abano Pass road to Tusheti is open.

9. **Abano Pass → Tusheti** (1–2 days)
   - ⚠️ Abano Pass (2,826 m) usually opens late May/early June — snowmelt dependent. **If closed, skip Tusheti.**
   - ~70 km from Alvani with 2,100 m climbing. Famous hairpin switchbacks, no guardrails.
   - Arrive in Omalo — main settlement. Watchtowers, guesthouses.

10. **Tusheti exploration** (1–2 days)
    - Day rides to Dartlo (best-preserved watchtower village, 15 km each way) and Diklo.
    - ⚠️ **Tushetian sheepdogs are genuinely dangerous.** Give herds 100 m+ berth. Walk slowly.

11. **Omalo → Alvani → Tbilisi** (1 day)
    - Descend Abano Pass or arrange 4×4 transfer from Omalo (common and cheap).
    - Marshrutka or ride to Tbilisi (~150 km).

12. **Fly Tbilisi → Turkey border area**

**If Abano Pass is closed:** Skip Tusheti entirely. Extend time in the Khevsureti valleys around Shatili, or head to Tbilisi early and fly to Turkey. Georgia delivers regardless — Tusheti is a bonus, not the trip.

## Key Logistics

- **Water:** Mountain springs everywhere. Not a concern.
- **Food:** Carry 2–3 days' supplies for the Barisakho–Shatili–Tusheti section. Guesthouses in Omalo serve meals.
- **Wild camping:** Easy and accepted. Nobody cares.
- **Sheepdogs:** Real hazard in Tusheti specifically. Give herds 100 m+ berth. Don't run. Walk slowly.

## Nature & Drawing Highlights

Truso Valley travertine terraces, Caucasus alpine flora (rhododendrons, gentians), volcanic plateaus, medieval stone architecture.
