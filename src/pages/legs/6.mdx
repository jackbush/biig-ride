**Enters Schengen.** By mid-August you have ~30 Schengen days available — enough for this section plus the start of the Alps.

## Route: Bari → Abruzzo → Train to Turin

1. **Train Bari → L'Aquila/Sulmona** (half day)
   - Skip the flat Puglia coast. No off-road interest. Save days and legs for the mountains.

2. **The Wolf's Lair Loop** (bikepacking.com) — 388 km, 8,500 m climbing
   - Starts/ends near L'Aquila
   - Links three National Parks: Gran Sasso, Maiella, Abruzzo
   - **Campo Imperatore plateau:** nicknamed "Italy's Little Tibet" — vast high-altitude grassland at 1,800 m with Corno Grande (2,912 m) behind it. Incredible photography.
   - Maiella massif: ancient tholos (stone shepherd shelters), deep canyons
   - Abruzzo National Park: the oldest in Italy. Marsican brown bears, Apennine wolves, chamois
   - Gravel doubletrack, mostly rideable, not very technical
   - Wild camping tolerated in the parks. Water fountains frequent.
   - **Must stop:** Ristoro Mucciante on the Gran Sasso plateau for arrosticini (lamb skewers) and pecorino

3. **L'Aquila → Train to Turin** (half day)
   - Direct train, bikes accepted on regional services (€3.50 supplement)

## Nature & Drawing Highlights

Campo Imperatore is extraordinary for landscape photography/drawing — stark plateau with dramatic mountain backdrop. Maiella has fascinating geology (karst, fossil-rich limestone). The wolf/bear presence makes the ecology feel genuinely wild — you may see wolves at dawn.

⚠️ **Wolves:** Real presence here, especially in the Maiella-Abruzzo corridor. They avoid humans but travel in packs. No recorded attacks on humans in Italy. Store food properly. More of an atmospheric thrill than a real danger.
