**Arrives Turin by train from L'Aquila.** This is the approach leg — get through the flat Po Plain and into the Venetian Prealps as fast as possible.

## Route: Turin → Veneto

- **Turin → east across the Po Plain** (1–2 days)
  - Flat, fast, dull. Cycle infrastructure is good. Keep it short.
  - The plain is 60–80 km wide here. Push through it.

- **Cut into the Venetian Prealps** (2–3 days)
  - The mountains start sharply east of Verona
  - Target the start of the Veneto Trail or the southern approach to the Dolomites
  - Towns: Bassano del Grappa, Feltre, Belluno area
  - The Prealps offer a gentle warm-up before the Dolomites proper

## Notes

By now you'll have been riding for ~3 months. The body is tuned. This short leg is intentionally easy — a transition, not a destination. Use it to:
- Check and replace drivetrain components (chain, brake pads) before the big mountains
- Get a full service or at least a good clean in Turin or Verona
- Eat well, rest, and arrive at the Dolomites fresh

## Into Leg 8

The Dolomites start abruptly — the transition from the flat Veneto to the rock towers is sudden and dramatic. Brixen/Bressanone is the standard start point for the Trans-Dolomiti.
