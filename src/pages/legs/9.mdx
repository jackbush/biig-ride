## Route: Dolomites → South Tyrol → Engadin → Swiss Alps

### Stelvio Area

From the Dolomites, head west through South Tyrol into Austria, then cross into Switzerland.

- **Stelvio via Lago di Cancano:** The unpaved Stelvio route via Lago di Cancano is a classic — old military road at altitude. Spectacular.
- **Inn Valley → Engadin (Switzerland):** Cross the border near Nauders/Scuol

### Swiss Alpine Bike Route (Route 1)

Switzerland's premier MTB route, ~680 km from east to west. Purpose-built singletrack in valleys, old trading routes over passes. Well-signed, Swiss Mobility app for navigation.

**Highlights:**
- Val Mora, Pass da Costainas
- Splügen area
- Bernina area (glacier views)
- Some sections are hike-a-bike at high passes

## Key Logistics

⚠️ **Austria:** Trail access rules are strict. Stick to designated routes — fines for off-trail riding are enforced. Much more relaxed in Italy and Switzerland.

⚠️ **Swiss wild camping:** Technically restricted in many cantons, but bivouacking above treeline (sleeping without a tent, or with just a bivvy) is generally tolerated. Be discreet, leave no trace.

⚠️ **Budget:** Swiss infrastructure is impeccable but **expensive** — €30–40/day minimum even wild camping. Resupply at Lidl/Migros in larger towns.

**Water:** Fountains everywhere in Switzerland. Springs reliable above treeline.

**Navigation:** Swiss Mobility app is essential — download maps offline before entry.
