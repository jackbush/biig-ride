**Border crossing:** Tbilisi → Posof/Türkgözü (or bus to border, ~6 hrs). Straightforward for bikes.
**Visa:** 90 days visa-free (NZ passport).

## Section A: Kaçkar Mountains (~2 weeks)

The Kaçkars are the Pontic Alps — a range parallel to the Black Sea coast, rising to 3,937 m. Northern slopes are lush (tea plantations, temperate rainforest); southern slopes are drier and more alpine.

**Route concept:**
- Cross into Turkey at Posof → ride south to Artvin area
- Climb into the Kaçkar range via yayla (summer pasture) roads — rough dirt/gravel 4×4 tracks connecting highland villages
- Key areas: Barhal Valley, Yaylalar, Olgunlar, Ayder
- Cross the range north–south via the Kavron or Elevit yayla roads
- Drop to the Black Sea coast briefly for resupply at Çamlıhemşin or Rize (tea country)
- Head west along the highland route, staying high on forest roads

**What to expect:**
- Yayla roads are rough 4×4 tracks — perfect for a hardtail, often impassable for cars
- Alpine lakes, glacial cirques, dense Colchic forests (ancient relict vegetation)
- Tea plantations on the lower slopes — unique landscape
- Shepherds at high yayla will offer you çay and maybe food
- Mountain springs everywhere
- Much cooler than the lowlands in June

⚠️ **Key hazards:** Yayla roads can wash out — check locally. Afternoon thunderstorms at altitude. Carry 2 days' food for high crossings.

## Section B: Anatolia Transit (~1 week)

The central Anatolian plateau is 1,000–1,500 m, hot in late June/July, and mostly flat agricultural land with limited off-road interest.

**Recommendation:** Train from Trabzon or Erzurum to Kayseri (Cappadocia's nearest city). Turkish trains carry bikes. Saves 5–7 days of dull, hot plateau riding.

## Section C: Cappadocia (~1 week)

**Route: Cappadocia Delight** (bikepacking.com) — ~400 km loop with significant off-road. Volcanic tuff landscape — fairy chimneys, underground cities, cave churches.

**Key highlights:**
- Göreme: fairy chimney epicentre, open-air cave church museum
- Ihlara Canyon: deep gorge with rock-cut churches, walkable/rideable canyon floor
- Derinkuyu/Kaymakli: underground cities carved 8 levels deep
- Aladağlar National Park views from the eastern section
- Mix of gravel roads, singletrack, some sandy sections (deflate tires), and tarmac connections

World-class geology. Volcanic tuff erosion pillars. Rock-cut architecture. Wild turtles on the eastern section. The light at dawn/dusk on the fairy chimneys is extraordinary for photography.

## Section D: Western Turkey to Greek Border (~1 week)

From Cappadocia, ride northwest toward Istanbul/Thrace area:
- Train from Kayseri/Nevşehir to Istanbul (saves 4–5 days)
- Then ride through Thrace to the Greek border at İpsala or Kastanies

The Thrace section is flat and agricultural — fast riding, not exciting. Push through it.

## Budget

Turkey is extremely cheap on the lira. Lokanta meals for €2–3. Wild camping easy and accepted.
