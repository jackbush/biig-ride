**The finish.** Cross from Switzerland into France and ride to the Mediterranean.

Cross from Switzerland via Grand St Bernard, or further south via Simplon/Nufenen.

## French Alps

- **Queyras Regional Park:** High-altitude valleys, military fortifications, excellent gravel
- **Mercantour National Park:** The most wild and rugged of the French Alpine parks
- **Briançon → Col d'Izoard:** Grand Tour climb, lunar landscape at the summit

## TNR Off-Road Sections

These are the sections from the previous Torino-Nice Rally attempt that need to be done properly off-road.

1. **Strada dell'Assietta** — 30 km+ of high-altitude military road along the ridge at ~2,500 m. Dusty gravel, massive views. Motorbike traffic can be annoying but the views are worth it.

2. **Strada dei Cannoni** — Rough, rocky military road. Descent into Valle Maira.

3. **"Little Peru" (Strada Gardetta)** — The highlight. 16 km of high-altitude gravel plateau at 2,300–2,500 m, rolling through alpine pasture below Rocca la Meja. Piedmontese cows, kestrels, virtually no people. Camp up here for sunrise.

4. **Via del Sale** — Ancient salt road near Col de Tende, running along the French-Italian border. Remote, high-altitude, excellent wild camping.

5. **Col de Tende descent** — 48 hairpins into the Roya Valley. Re-routed due to Storm Alex flood damage — check current TNR GPX for updated sections.

## Finish: Nice

Ride down to the coast via Col de Turini or similar. Mediterranean landscape takes over — drier, more scrub, dramatically different from the high Alps just hours behind.

## Decision Point

- **Schengen days exhausted? Embassy confirmed bilateral?** → Ride on in France (Pyrenees, Provence, etc.) on the additional 90 days. Stay in metropolitan France only.
- **No bilateral confirmation?** → Fly Nice → NZ. Good connections to major hubs from Nice airport.

⚠️ **Late season weather:** High passes (2,500 m+) can get early snow mid-October. TNR sections (Assietta, Little Peru) at 2,300–2,500 m — check forecasts daily from late September. Have bail-out plans for each high section.
