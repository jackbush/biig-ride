**Duration:** ~5 months (mid-May to late October 2026, with France bilateral extension possible)  
**Bike:** Hardtail MTB, 2.2"+ tires, bikepacking bags  
**Budget:** €15–25/day

<h2 id="leg-1">LEG 1: GEORGIA — Caucasus Highlights (2 weeks, ~May 18 – Jun 1)</h2>

**Fly:** London → Tbilisi (Wizz Air, typically £50-100)

### Route: Eastern Caucasus Highlight Loop
~500-600km, ~15,000m climbing

**Day-by-day framework:**

1. **Tbilisi → Kazbegi (Stepantsminda)** via Georgian Military Highway (2 days)
   - Take the highway north — it's paved but spectacular. Jvari Monastery overlook. Ananuri fortress.
   - Arrive Stepantsminda with Kazbek (5,047m) towering above.

2. **Kazbegi area explorations** (2-3 days)
   - **Truso Valley:** Dirt road to a travertine plateau with mineral springs and abandoned villages. Stunning geology — calcium carbonate deposits, sulphur springs. Excellent for drawing/photos.
   - **Juta Valley → Chaukhi Pass (3,338m):** Hike-a-bike over the pass. This is the committing section — steep snow possible in late May. River crossings. Check conditions locally before attempting.
   - Camp at altitude, shepherd tracks, alpine meadows.

3. **Kazbegi → Shatili** (2-3 days)
   - Remote military road over Datvisjvari Pass. This section is wild — minimal habitation, exposed terrain.
   - Shatili: medieval fortified village complex, extraordinary stone towers.
   - Register at army checkpoint (required for the hiking trail areas near the Chechen border).

4. **Shatili → Tusheti (Omalo)** (2-3 days)
   - Via Abano Pass (2,826m) — the famous "most dangerous road" with hairpins and no guardrails. Spectacular but manageable on a bike.
   - Tusheti is only accessible ~June–October. Confirm road is open before committing.
   - Omalo: stone watchtowers, end-of-the-world feel. Guesthouses with incredible food.

5. **Tusheti → Tbilisi** (2 days)
   - Descend via Abano Pass road back to Alvani, then road to Tbilisi.
   - Or arrange a shared 4x4 transfer from Omalo (common and cheap) to save time.

**Key logistics:**
- **Water:** Mountain springs everywhere. Not a concern.
- **Food:** Carry 2-3 days' supplies for the Shatili–Tusheti section. Guesthouses in Omalo serve meals.
- **Wild camping:** Easy and accepted. Nobody cares.
- **Sheepdogs:** Real hazard. Give herds 100m+ berth. Don't run. Walk slowly.
- **Season:** Late May is early — high passes may have snow. Truso/Juta area opens earlier than Atsunta. Abano Pass usually opens by late May but check.

**Nature/drawing highlights:** Truso Valley travertine terraces, Caucasus alpine flora (rhododendrons, gentians), volcanic plateaus, medieval stone architecture.

<h2 id="leg-2">LEG 2: TURKEY — Kaçkars & Cappadocia (5 weeks, ~Jun 1 – Jul 10)</h2>

**Border crossing:** Tbilisi → Posof/Türkgözü border (or bus to the border, ~6hrs). Straightforward for bikes.

**Visa:** 90 days visa-free (NZ passport).

### Section A: Kaçkar Mountains (~2 weeks)

The Kaçkars are the Pontic Alps — a range running parallel to the Black Sea coast, rising to 3,937m. The northern slopes are lush (tea plantations, temperate rainforest); the southern slopes are drier and more alpine.

**Route concept:**
- Cross into Turkey at Posof → ride south to Artvin area
- Climb into the Kaçkar range via yayla (summer pasture) roads — these are rough dirt/gravel 4x4 tracks connecting highland villages
- Key areas: Barhal Valley, Yaylalar, Olgunlar, Ayder
- Cross the range north-south via the Kavron or Elevit yayla roads
- Drop to the Black Sea coast briefly for resupply at Çamlıhemşin or Rize (tea country)
- Head west along the highland route, staying high on forest roads

**What to expect:**
- Yayla roads are rough 4x4 tracks — perfect for your hardtail, often impassable for cars
- Alpine lakes, glacial cirques, dense Colchic forests (ancient relict vegetation from the Tertiary)
- **Tea plantations** on the lower slopes — unique landscape
- Shepherds at high yayla will offer you çay and maybe food
- Mountain springs everywhere — water not a concern
- Much cooler than the lowlands in June

**⚠️ Key hazards:**
- Yayla roads can wash out — check locally
- Afternoon thunderstorms at altitude
- Limited resupply above ~1,500m — carry 2 days' food for high crossings

### Section B: Transit across Anatolia (~1 week)

From the Kaçkars you need to get southwest to Cappadocia. The central Anatolian plateau is 1,000-1,500m, hot in late June/July, and mostly flat agricultural land with limited off-road interest.

**Recommendation:** Train from Trabzon or Erzurum to Kayseri (Cappadocia's nearest city). Turkish trains carry bikes. This saves 5-7 days of dull, hot plateau riding.

### Section C: Cappadocia (~1 week)

**Route: Cappadocia Delight** (bikepacking.com)
~400km loop with significant off-road. Volcanic tuff landscape — fairy chimneys, underground cities, cave churches.

**Key highlights:**
- Göreme: fairy chimney epicentre, open-air cave church museum
- Ihlara Canyon: deep gorge with rock-cut churches, walkable/rideable canyon floor
- Derinkuyu/Kaymakli: underground cities carved 8 levels deep
- Aladağlar National Park views from the eastern section
- Mix of gravel roads, singletrack, some sandy sections (deflate tires), and tarmac connections

**Nature/drawing:** World-class geology. Volcanic tuff erosion pillars. Rock-cut architecture. Wild turtles on the eastern section. The light at dawn/dusk on the fairy chimneys is extraordinary for photography.

**Budget:** Turkey is extremely cheap on the lira. Lokanta meals for €2-3. Wild camping easy and accepted.

### Section D: Western Turkey to Greek border (~1 week)

From Cappadocia, ride northwest toward Istanbul/Thrace area. Several options:
- Train from Kayseri/Nevşehir to Istanbul (saves 4-5 days)
- Then ride through Thrace to the Greek border at İpsala or Kastanies

The Thrace section is flat and agricultural — fast riding, not exciting. Push through it.

<h2 id="leg-3">LEG 3: GREECE TRANSIT (3 days, ~Jul 12-15)</h2>

**Border:** İpsala (Turkey) → Kipi (Greece). Straightforward for bikes.

**Route:** Kipi → ride west through Thrace → cross into Albania at Kakavija or Krystallopigi.

~300-400km of flat-to-rolling agricultural land. Ride it fast — every day costs a Schengen day. The interesting mountains (Pindus, Zagori) are worth it but cost extra days you can't spare.

**Alternative:** If you have time pressure, bus from the border area to Thessaloniki or Ioannina, then ride to Albania.

**Schengen days used: 3**

<h2 id="leg-4">LEG 4: ALBANIA — The Accursed Mountains (2 weeks, ~Jul 15 – Aug 1)</h2>

**Non-Schengen.** 90 days visa-free.

### Route: Northern Albanian Alps Loop

**Key sections:**

1. **Entry from Greece → Korçë / Pogradec area** (if entering from the south)
   Or: Greece → Ioannina area → Albania via Kakavija border

2. **Shkodër → Theth** (2-3 days)
   - The climb from Shkodër to Theth is the signature Albanian bikepacking experience
   - Paved for first 20km, then gravel/mud. 2.2"+ tires essential.
   - Theth: dramatic limestone valley, waterfall, lock-in tower (blood feud history)

3. **Theth → Valbona** (1-2 days)
   - Via Valbona Pass (1,795m) — classic Peaks of the Balkans hiking trail
   - Hike-a-bike over the pass, but rideable sections either side
   - Valbona Valley: ancient beech forests (UNESCO), crystal-clear river

4. **Lake Koman Ferry** (1 day)
   - Ride to the ferry at Fierze, take the boat through the flooded canyon
   - One of Europe's most scenic boat rides
   - Emerge at Koman, ride to Shkodër

5. **Shkodër → Montenegro** (1 day)
   - Border crossing at Hani i Hotit or Muriqan

**Inspiration routes:**
- **Illyrian Loop** (Seven Serpents): 650km loop through northern Albania
- **The Accursed Race** route (Lost Dot): 1,600km through Albania/Montenegro/Bosnia/Kosovo

**Budget:** €10-15/day. Guesthouse + dinner = €15. Raki is offered freely.

**⚠️ Stray dogs** are more aggressive than anywhere else on this route. Carry stones. A walking stick helps.

**Nature/drawing:** Albanian Alps are karst limestone — sheer white peaks, turquoise rivers, endemic Balkan flora. Ophiolite belt (exposed ocean crust rock) in central Albania — geologically rare and visually striking (dark serpentinite among white limestone).

<h2 id="leg-5">LEG 5: MONTENEGRO — Durmitor (10 days, ~Aug 1-12)</h2>

**Non-Schengen.** 90 days visa-free.

### Route: Mountains to Coast

1. **Enter from Albania** near Shkodër → ride north through Prokletije (Accursed Mountains, Montenegro side)
2. **Durmitor National Park** (3-4 days)
   - Limestone massif, glacial lakes (Black Lake is stunning), dense forest
   - Excellent dirt roads and 4x4 tracks through the park
   - Wild camping at altitude is the way to do this
   - **Tara River Canyon:** deepest in Europe (1,300m). Ride the rim road.
3. **South to the coast** (2-3 days)
   - Via Lovćen National Park (hairpin descent to Kotor Bay)
   - Or more directly to Bar for the ferry

**Ferry:** Bar → Bari (Italy). Overnight, ~9hrs. ~€40-60 with bike.

**Nature:** Old-growth black pine forests, griffon vultures overhead. Bears and wolves present but extremely shy — not a real concern. Standard food-hanging practice is sufficient.

<h2 id="leg-6">LEG 6: APENNINE HIGHLIGHTS (10-12 days, ~Aug 13-25)</h2>

**Enters Schengen.** By mid-August you have ~30 Schengen days available — enough for this section plus the start of the Alps.

### Route: Bari → Abruzzo → Train to Turin

1. **Train Bari → L'Aquila/Sulmona** (half day)
   - Skip the flat Puglia coast. No off-road interest. Save your days and legs for the mountains.

2. **The Wolf's Lair Loop** (bikepacking.com) — 388km, 8,500m climbing
   - Starts/ends near L'Aquila
   - Links three National Parks: Gran Sasso, Maiella, Abruzzo
   - **Campo Imperatore plateau:** nicknamed "Italy's Little Tibet" — vast high-altitude grassland at 1,800m with Corno Grande (2,912m) behind it. Incredible photography.
   - Maiella massif: ancient tholos (stone shepherd shelters), deep canyons
   - Abruzzo National Park: the oldest in Italy. Marsican brown bears, Apennine wolves, chamois
   - Gravel doubletrack, mostly rideable, not very technical
   - Wild camping tolerated in the parks. Water fountains frequent.
   - **Must stop:** Ristoro Mucciante on the Gran Sasso plateau for arrosticini (lamb skewers) and pecorino

3. **L'Aquila → Train to Turin** (half day)
   - Direct train, bikes accepted on regional services (€3.50 supplement)

**Nature/drawing:** Campo Imperatore is extraordinary for landscape photography/drawing — stark plateau with dramatic mountain backdrop. Maiella has fascinating geology (karst, fossil-rich limestone). The wolf/bear presence makes the ecology feel genuinely wild — you may see wolves at dawn.

**⚠️ Wolves:** Real presence here, especially in the Maiella-Abruzzo corridor. They avoid humans but travel in packs. No recorded attacks on humans in Italy, but store food properly. More of an atmospheric thrill than a real danger.

<h2 id="leg-7">LEG 7: THE ALPS — Counterclockwise Loop (~45 days, ~Aug 26 – Oct 10)</h2>

This is the centrepiece. A counterclockwise circuit of the Alps:

**Turin → southeast to Veneto → Trans-Dolomiti → east into Austria → Swiss Alps heading west → French Alps south → TNR sections → Nice**

~2,500-3,000km with enormous climbing. By now you'll be very fit (3+ months in the saddle).

### Section A: Piedmont → Veneto Approach (4-5 days)

- Turin → ride east across the Po Plain (flat, fast, dull — keep it short)
- Cut into the Venetian Prealps as soon as possible
- Target: the start of the **Veneto Trail** or the southern approach to the Dolomites

### Section B: Dolomites (10-14 days)

**Primary route: Trans-Dolomiti** (bikepacking.com, by Tales on Tyres)
262km loop, 9,529m climbing. Ride clockwise from Brixen.

- Old WWI military roads (Strade Militari) over high passes
- Rocky dirt tracks, singletrack sections, alpine meadows between colossal limestone towers
- Hut-to-hut infrastructure available but wild camping is more flexible in Italy
- The Dolomite geology is extraordinary: Triassic carbonate reef platforms, now standing as sheer towers. Enrosadira effect — the rock turns pink/orange at dawn and dusk.

**Add-ons to consider:**
- **Zillertal Trail** (bikepacking.com): 177km loop, three passes connecting Italian and Austrian Zillertal Alps. Start from Brunico (on the Trans-Dolomiti route).
- **No Borders Bikepacking** route: Through Julian Alps, Karawanks, Carnic Alps (Austria/Italy/Slovenia border). Extends east but adds Schengen days in Slovenia.

**Key logistics:**
- Mid-September timing is ideal: fewer hikers, passes still open, autumn colours starting
- Espresso and pasta at every resupply town
- Water from mountain streams and village fountains

### Section C: Austrian Alps → Swiss Alps (10-14 days)

**Route concept:** From the Dolomites, head west through South Tyrol into Austria, then cross into Switzerland.

- **Stelvio area:** The unpaved Stelvio route via Lago di Cancano is a classic — old military road.
- **Inn Valley → Engadin (Switzerland):** Cross the border near Nauders/Scuol
- **Swiss Alpine Bike Route (Route 1):** Switzerland's premier MTB route, ~680km from east to west. Purpose-built singletrack in valleys, old trading routes over passes. Well-signed, Swiss Mobility app for navigation.
  - Highlights: Val Mora, Pass da Costainas, Splügen area, Bernina area (glacier views)
  - Some sections are hike-a-bike at high passes
  - Swiss infrastructure is impeccable but **expensive** (€30-40/day minimum)

**⚠️ Austria:** Trail access rules are strict. Stick to designated routes — fines for off-trail riding are enforced. Much more relaxed in Italy and Switzerland.

**⚠️ Swiss wild camping:** Technically restricted in many cantons, but bivouacking above treeline (sleeping without a tent, or with just a bivvy) is generally tolerated. Be discreet, leave no trace.

### Section D: French Alps → TNR Sections → Nice (10-14 days)

Cross from Switzerland into France (e.g., via Grand St Bernard, or further south via Simplon/Nufenen).

**French Alps highlights:**
- Queyras Regional Park: high-altitude valleys, military fortifications, excellent gravel
- Mercantour National Park: the most wild and rugged of the French Alpine parks
- Briançon → Col d'Izoard: Grand Tour climb, lunar landscape at the summit

**TNR Off-Road Sections (the ones you want to redo properly):**

1. **Strada dell'Assietta** — 30km+ of high-altitude military road along the ridge at ~2,500m. Dusty gravel, massive views. Motorbike traffic can be annoying but the views are worth it.

2. **Strada dei Cannoni** — Rough, rocky military road. Descent into Valle Maira.

3. **"Little Peru" (Strada Gardetta)** — The highlight. 16km of high-altitude gravel plateau at 2,300-2,500m, rolling through alpine pasture below Rocca la Meja. Piedmontese cows, kestrels, virtually no people. Camp up here for sunrise.

4. **Via del Sale** — Ancient salt road near Col de Tende, running along the French-Italian border. Remote, high-altitude, excellent wild camping.

5. **Col de Tende descent** — 48 hairpins into the Roya Valley. Now well worth re-routing due to Storm Alex flood damage (check current TNR GPX for updated sections).

**Finish in Nice.** Ride down to the coast via Col de Turini or similar. Mediterranean landscape takes over — drier, more scrub, dramatically different from the high Alps just hours behind you.

## Kit Considerations (brief)

- **Tires:** Bring a spare folding tire. Remote Turkey and Albania have zero MTB tire availability.
- **Chain/cassette:** Consider a mid-tour replacement in a Dolomites bike shop (Brixen, Bolzano). By the Alps you'll have 4,000km+ on the drivetrain.
- **Brake pads:** Carry 2 spare sets. The Alps will destroy them.
- **Stove:** Essential for Georgia, Turkey, Albania wild camping sections. Optional in the Alps (more refugio/town access).
- **Water:** 2x 750ml bottles + 1-2L soft flask for Turkey's dry sections. Mountains everywhere else have springs.
- **Navigation:** Komoot + Gaia GPS + Maps.me (offline maps essential for Turkey/Balkans). Swiss Mobility app for Switzerland.
